What is IoT in Construction?

The Internet of Things (IoT) in construction refers to the use of smart devices, sensors, and connected technologies to monitor, manage, and improve construction activities in real time. These devices collect data such as temperature, humidity, vibration, and worker movement, which is then transmitted to cloud platforms or web dashboards for analysis.


IoT helps construction companies increase safety, efficiency, and productivity. For example, wearable devices like smart helmets can detect accidents and send alerts, while sensors can monitor structural conditions and environmental factors. This reduces risks, prevents delays, and improves decision-making.


Overall, IoT is transforming the construction industry by enabling smarter, data-driven operations and creating safer working environments.
